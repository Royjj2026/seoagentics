Common questions

Pricing questions, answered.

The things agencies ask most before booking a call — answered honestly.

Why isn't there a fixed price on the page?
Because the product isn't the same for every agency. Two agencies with ten clients each might have completely different workflow complexity, content volume, and customisation requirements — and quoting them the same number would either undercharge one or overcharge the other. The model is transparent, the variables are honest, and the number is agreed before anything starts.
What does the setup fee actually cover?
Two things: platform-level configuration (your agency's workflow, briefing process, output format, approval steps) and per-client engine builds (brand training, ICP alignment, style matching, knowledge bases). Both are one-time costs. The platform configuration happens once. Each new client engine is a per-client setup fee at the time of onboarding.
How does the monthly retainer scale as we add clients?
The retainer is per client, per month — starting from $25 per client based on content volume. As your portfolio grows, volume discounts apply to the per-client rate. Larger rosters cost less per client, not more. The economics are designed to reward growth rather than penalise it.
What happens if we want to add a new client mid-engagement?
New clients are added as new engine builds at the per-client setup rate. Once built, they're added to your monthly retainer at the current per-client rate for your portfolio size. There's no renegotiation, no tier change — it's additive and predictable.
Is there a minimum number of clients required?
There's no hard minimum, but SEO Agentics is designed for agencies managing five or more clients. Below that, the economics of a bespoke build may not be the right fit — and we'll be honest about that on the call rather than take an engagement that isn't right for you.
Can the retainer be split across clients or billed per client?
Both options are available. Some agencies prefer a single monthly invoice across the full roster. Others prefer per-client billing so costs can be allocated or passed through to clients. We'll agree the structure that works for your agency during the setup conversation.
